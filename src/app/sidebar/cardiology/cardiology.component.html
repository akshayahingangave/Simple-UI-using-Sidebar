Choosing a cardiologist

Patients are often referred to a cardiologist by their physician, but they may wish to choose their own specialist. The American Heart Association (AHA) advises people to check that their cardiologist is board certified. Patients can check this through the American Board of Internal Medicine (ABIM) or the American Board of Family Medicine (ABFM). It is also important to choose a doctor whose communication style suits them. If the cardiologist recommends a treatment that seems risky or expensive, or if the patient is not sure why they need this treatment, the AHA suggest looking for a second opinion.
