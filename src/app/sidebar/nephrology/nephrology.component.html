When to see a nephrologist

A family or emergency doctor may refer anyone with signs or symptoms of chronic, moderate, or severe kidney dysfunction to a nephrologist. They will usually refer babies born with kidney dysfunction to a nephrologist immediately. Some common signs and symptoms of severe kidney problems include:

  • swelling, often in the legs, ankles, or feet
  • itchiness
  • reduced sense of taste and appetite
  • unintentional weight loss
  • unexplained confusion, memory problems, or trouble focusing
  • pain, fluid in the joints, or stiffness
